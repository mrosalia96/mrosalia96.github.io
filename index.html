Hi, I'm Michael Rosalia! I'm a six-year US Navy Veteran (E-5) currently pursuing a Bachelor's in Computer Science with a minor in Business Administration at the University of San Diego. My unique combination of military experience, technical skills, and business knowledge gives me a well-rounded perspective on solving complex problems.

My journey spans from leading RF troubleshooting operations as an Electronics Technician in the Navy to exploring the world of data engineering. At Forward Slope, Inc., I worked as an Installation Engineer where I traveled internationally to countries like Azerbaijan to install complex sensor and communication systems. This role allowed me to collaborate directly with host countries' military personnel, conduct site surveys for optimal system architecture, and create detailed technical documentation for critical systems.

I'm particularly passionate about the data science field, where I can combine analytical thinking with programming to extract meaningful insights from complex datasets. My recent work as a Data Engineer Intern at a Financial Engineering Firm allowed me to design custom financial data solutions that reduced manual processing time by 70% while ensuring complete accuracy in financial reports.

My Computer Science coursework in Data Structures, Algorithms, and Data Science Foundations has strengthened my technical skills, while my Business Administration minor provides me with valuable context on how technology solutions impact organizational objectives and business processes. This interdisciplinary approach helps me bridge the gap between technical implementation and business value.

Beyond academics and professional work, I maintain an active wellness-based lifestyle through a variety of physical activities. I'm an avid cyclist, regularly practicing Jiu Jitsu, and incorporating yoga into my routine for balance and flexibility. When I have free time, I also enjoy golfing.

I enjoy developing personal projects that combine my interests in technology and well-being, such as my meditation application that facilitates Wim Hof breathing exercises. I actively participate in the tech community through my memberships with the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM), Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE), and Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE).
